## Grammar & Style Guidelines

### Formality Level (Tu vs. Vous)

**Use "vous" (formal) for:**
- Professional/business software (B2B)
- Financial services (banking, insurance)
- Healthcare applications
- Government services
- Legal services
- Enterprise products
- Professional tools
- Educational platforms (formal)

**Use "tu" (informal) for:**
- Consumer/casual apps (B2C)
- Social media platforms
- Gaming applications
- Youth-focused products
- Dating apps
- Fitness/lifestyle apps
- Apps with friendly, casual brand voice
- Entertainment products

**Examples:**
- Formal: "Connectez-vous à votre compte"
- Informal: "Connecte-toi à ton compte"

**Mixed approach:**
Some products use "vous" in legal/security contexts but "tu" elsewhere. This should be carefully considered for consistency.

### Gender Neutrality

French is a gendered language, but inclusive approaches exist:

**Traditional:**
- "utilisateur" (masculine generic)
- "administrateur" (masculine generic)

**Inclusive options:**
- "utilisateur·rice" or "utilisateur.rice" (point médian)
- "utilisateur ou utilisatrice" (explicit)
- "personne utilisatrice" (feminine noun, works for all)
- Prefer role-based terms when possible: "gestionnaire" (works for all genders)

**Canadian vs European:**
- Quebec is more conservative with inclusive writing
- France more open to point médian and other forms
- Know your audience and their preferences

### Canadian French (Québécois) Specifics

**Avoid anglicisms (enforced by OQLF):**
- ❌ "chat" → ✅ "clavardage"
- ❌ "e-mail" → ✅ "courriel"
- ❌ "uploader" → ✅ "téléverser"
- ❌ "forwarder" → ✅ "transférer"
- ❌ "logger" → ✅ "se connecter"
- ❌ "timer" → ✅ "minuteur, minuterie"
- ❌ "software" → ✅ "logiciel"

**Use Canadian spelling:**
- "courriel" (Quebec) vs "courrier électronique" (France)
- "magasinage" (Quebec) vs "shopping" (France accepts this)
- "stationnement" (Quebec) vs "parking" (France accepts this)
- "fin de semaine" (Quebec) vs "week-end" (France)

**Regulatory compliance:**
- Follow OQLF Grand dictionnaire terminologique
- Quebec Law 101: French must be default for Quebec users
- Quebec Law 25: Privacy requirements with French interface
- French version must be equal or superior quality to English

### European French Specifics

**More accepting of anglicisms:**
- "e-mail" or "mail" (widely accepted)
- "week-end" (accepted)
- "parking" (accepted)
- "shopping" (accepted in marketing)
- "planning" (used for schedules)

**Tech terminology:**
- More flexible with tech terms
- "appli" for "app" (accepted short form)
- "tchat" for "chat" (accepted)
- "smartphone" (accepted)

### Capitalization

**Both variants:**
- Buttons: Capitalize first word only
  - ✅ "Se connecter"
  - ❌ "Se Connecter"
- Titles/headings: Capitalize first word only
  - ✅ "Tableau de bord"
  - ❌ "Tableau De Bord"
- Proper nouns: Capitalize as appropriate
- Acronyms: Full caps (FAQ, PDF)

### Punctuation

**Spaces before punctuation (both variants):**
- Add non-breaking space before: `:` `;` `?` `!` `%`
- No space before: `.` `,`

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Êtes-vous sûr ?"
- ❌ "Êtes-vous sûr?"
- ✅ "Attention : cette action est irréversible."
- ✅ "Taux : 15 %"

**Quotation marks:**
- Canadian: Often uses "English style"
- European: Uses « guillemets français » with spaces
  - ✅ « Bonjour »
  - ❌ «Bonjour»

### Numbers & Currency

**Decimal separator:**
- Use comma: "3,14" (not "3.14")
- Both variants use comma

**Thousands separator:**
- Use space: "1 000" or "10 000" (not "1,000")
- Or no separator for 4 digits: "1000" is acceptable

**Currency (Canadian):**
- Dollar: "10,99 $" (amount, space, symbol)
- Or: "10,99 dollars canadiens"

**Currency (European):**
- Euro: "10,99 €" (amount, space, symbol)
- Or: "10,99 euros"
- Symbol can come before in some contexts: "€ 10,99"

**Percentages:**
- Both: "50 %" (space before %)

**Dates:**
- Canadian: YYYY-MM-DD (ISO 8601) or DD-MM-YYYY
- European: DD/MM/YYYY more common
- Spell out: "15 janvier 2024" (both)
- Month names not capitalized in French

**Time:**
- 24-hour format: "14:30" (both)
- Spell out: "14 h 30" or "14 heures 30"

## Context-Specific Guidelines

### Button Labels

**Action-oriented with verbs:**
- ✅ "Enregistrer les modifications"
- ❌ "Enregistrement"
- ✅ "Créer un compte"
- ❌ "Création de compte"
- ✅ "Se connecter"
- ❌ "Connexion"

**Be specific:**
- ✅ "Enregistrer les modifications" (Save changes)
- ❌ "Enregistrer" alone (ambiguous - save what?)

**Primary actions:**
- Use strong, clear verbs
- "Confirmer", "Continuer", "Créer", "Envoyer"

**Secondary/destructive actions:**
- "Annuler", "Supprimer", "Ignorer"

### Error Messages

**Be specific and helpful:**
- ✅ "Le mot de passe doit contenir au moins 8 caractères."
- ❌ "Mot de passe invalide."
- ✅ "L'adresse courriel est déjà utilisée. Essayez de vous connecter ou utilisez une autre adresse."
- ❌ "Erreur."

**Structure:**
1. What went wrong (specific)
2. Why it happened (if helpful)
3. How to fix it (actionable)

**Tone:**
- Don't blame: ❌ "Vous avez entré..." → ✅ "L'adresse courriel..."
- Be empathetic: "Désolé, une erreur s'est produite."
- Offer solutions: "Veuillez vérifier votre connexion et réessayer."

### Empty States

**Be encouraging and action-oriented:**
- ✅ "Aucun fichier. Téléversez votre premier fichier pour commencer." (CA)
- ✅ "Aucun fichier. Téléchargez votre premier fichier pour commencer." (EU)
- ❌ "Vide."

**Structure:**
1. State what's empty
2. Explain why or what to do
3. Clear CTA

### Loading States

**Be clear about what's happening:**
- ✅ "Chargement de vos données..."
- ✅ "Envoi en cours..."
- ✅ "Téléversement... 45 %" (CA)
- ✅ "Téléchargement... 45 %" (EU for upload)
- ❌ "Chargement..." (too vague)

### Placeholders

**Use descriptive, helpful examples:**
- ✅ "Entrez votre adresse courriel" (CA)
- ✅ "Entrez votre adresse e-mail" (EU)
- ✅ "Rechercher par nom..."
- ✅ "Ex : Jean Tremblay"

## Common Patterns

### Confirmations

**Delete confirmations:**
```
Êtes-vous sûr de vouloir supprimer [item] ?
Cette action est irréversible.

[Supprimer] [Annuler]
```

**Leave confirmations:**
```
Vous avez des modifications non enregistrées.
Voulez-vous quitter sans enregistrer ?

[Quitter sans enregistrer] [Annuler]
```

**Destructive action confirmations:**
```
Cette action ne peut pas être annulée.
Tapez "[confirmation text]" pour confirmer.

[Confirmer] [Annuler]
```

## Regional Variants Comparison

### When to Use Canadian French
- Targeting Canadian market (especially Quebec)
- Required by Quebec regulations (Law 101)
- Canadian company/product
- Must use OQLF-approved terminology

### When to Use European French
- Targeting European market (France, Belgium, Switzerland, etc.)
- International French-speaking audience
- European company/product

### When to Support Both
- Global product with significant users in both regions
- Consider language selector: "Français (Canada)" vs "Français (France)"
- Maintain separate translation files if needed
- Key differences are minimal but important for UX

### Universal French
For minor features or where separate variants are not feasible:
- Avoid region-specific terms
- Use terminology common to both (most terms are identical)
- Test with users from both regions
- Key universal terms: most UI actions, navigation, common features

## Common Translation Mistakes

### 1. Literal Translation of Idioms
- ❌ "It's raining cats and dogs" → "Il pleut des chats et des chiens"
- ✅ "Il pleut des cordes" or "Il pleut à verse"

### 2. Anglicisms in Quebec Context
- ❌ "logger" → ✅ "se connecter"
- ❌ "e-mail" → ✅ "courriel" (Quebec)
- ❌ "forwarder" → ✅ "transférer"

### 3. Wrong Register
- ❌ Using "tu" in professional B2B context
- ❌ Using "vous" in casual gaming app

### 4. Ignoring Text Expansion
- Not budgeting for 15-30% longer French text
- Button labels that overflow

### 5. Wrong Context
- "Save" → depends on context:
  - "Enregistrer" (save file)
  - "Sauvegarder" (save backup)
  - "Économiser" (save money)

### 6. Gender Agreement Issues
- ❌ "Connecté" for female user
- ✅ Use gender-neutral constructs or dynamic: "Connecté·e"

### 7. Punctuation Errors
- ❌ "Êtes-vous sûr?" (missing space)
- ✅ "Êtes-vous sûr ?"

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing translations:

**Accuracy:**
- [ ] Meaning preserved from source
- [ ] Technical terms correct
- [ ] No mistranslations or false friends
- [ ] Context understood and applied

**Linguistic Quality:**
- [ ] Natural and fluent
- [ ] Grammar and syntax correct
- [ ] Appropriate formality (tu/vous) for context
- [ ] Proper spelling and accents (é, è, ê, à, etc.)
- [ ] Consistent terminology throughout

**Regional Appropriateness:**
- [ ] Correct variant (Canadian vs European)
- [ ] Complies with OQLF if targeting Quebec
- [ ] Avoids anglicisms where required
- [ ] Uses appropriate currency/date formats

**UX/UI:**
- [ ] Fits UI space constraints (test actual UI)
- [ ] Clear and actionable
- [ ] Action-oriented (buttons use verbs)
- [ ] Error messages provide solutions
- [ ] Consistent with platform conventions

**Technical:**
- [ ] Correct punctuation (spaces before :;?!)
- [ ] Proper number formatting (comma decimals)
- [ ] Correct date/time formats
- [ ] Currency symbols placed correctly
- [ ] Character encoding correct (UTF-8)
- [ ] Works with screen readers

**Cultural:**
- [ ] Culturally appropriate for target market
- [ ] Idioms adapted, not translated literally
- [ ] Examples use local references
- [ ] No cultural insensitivities

**Compliance:**
- [ ] Meets OQLF requirements (Quebec)
- [ ] Privacy policy translated if required
- [ ] Legal terms reviewed by legal team
- [ ] Regulatory requirements met

## When to Consult Native Speakers

Even with comprehensive guidelines, consult native speakers for:
- Marketing copy and slogans
- Culturally-specific content
- Humor and wordplay
- New product names
- Legal or compliance-critical text
- When in doubt about cultural appropriateness

**Testing:**
- Test with native speakers from target region
- A/B test different translations when possible
- Gather feedback from users
- Monitor support requests for confusion
